CONTEXT
================================================================

v8.1 adds macro-node compression to the virtual layer.
When --compress is passed, stable labels (age >= min_age,
nodes >= 3, internal links with S >= 0.20) are compressed
into MacroNode objects at a designated window. Their internal
links are removed from the physical layer. MacroNodes exert
torque via their state vector, not micro-physics.

Compression is OFF by default. Without --compress, v8.1
behaves identically to v8.0 (lifecycle logging only).

The equivalence test (run_equivalence.sh) runs compressed vs
uncompressed on the same seed for 500 windows and compares.

SECTION I: KNOWN RISKS
================================================================

RISK 1: CPU savings may be minimal.
  The bottleneck is realizer substrate scan (45%), not internal
  label link evaluation. Compressing ~20 internal links saves
  ~0.3%. The 5% CPU target in Gemini §4 is likely unachievable
  without substrate-scan optimization.
  REVIEWER: Flag if this invalidates the equivalence test.

RISK 2: _compute_perimeter_omega iterates all alive_l.
  O(alive_l) per macro-node per window. With 5 macro-nodes and
  3000 links = 15K iterations. Tolerable at N=5000 but scales
  linearly. Should use pre-built degree map for O(degree).
  REVIEWER: Flag as optimization opportunity.

RISK 3: Macro-node death releases nodes but does NOT restore
  removed internal links. Those links are permanently gone.
  If the macro-node dies and the nodes rejoin the system, they
  have fewer links than they would in the uncompressed case.
  REVIEWER: Assess whether this introduces systematic bias.

RISK 4: Maturation re-fit (Gemini §2) revealed only 10 mature
  survivors (age>=50) in 48 seeds at 200 windows. The 500-window
  equivalence test should produce more, but E1 parameters for
  the mature cohort are statistically weak.
  REVIEWER: Note in audit memo.
