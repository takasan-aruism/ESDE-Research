2. Implementation Decisions: Physics Engine Overhaul
------------------------------------------------------------
To transition from a homogeneous environment to a localized dynamic ecosystem, the following explicit design choices have been made in response to Implementation queries:

Decision 1: Replacement of Global Diffusion
The uniform global pressure model (`apply_diffusion`) is completely deprecated. It is replaced entirely by localized Wave Propagation. All external semantic injections will now enter the system as physical waves originating from specific nodes and propagating outward.

Decision 2: Wave Energy Mechanics (Destruction & Creation)
When a wave reaches a node, it applies a dual-effect based on its Amplitude and attenuation:
A. Phase (θ) Shift: Forced physical perturbation disrupting existing synchronization.
B. Link Strength (S) Stress: 
   - Destructive Regime: If the amplitude exceeds the structural threshold, existing links are forcefully severed (representing the destruction of rigid clusters).
   - Activation Regime: If the amplitude is within a micro-fluctuation band (e.g., attenuated at the wave's periphery), it temporarily increases the node's search radius and binding probability, fostering new connections and diversity.

Decision 3: Branch Isolation (No Backward Compatibility)
v4.1 represents a fundamental paradigm shift introducing emergent space. To maintain absolute codebase purity, v4.1 will be a clean hard fork. We will not maintain backward compatibility with v3.9/v4.0 homogeneous pressure experiments.
